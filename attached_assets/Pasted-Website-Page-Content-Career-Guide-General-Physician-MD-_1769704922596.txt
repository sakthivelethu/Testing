Who Is a General Physician (Internist)?

An Internal Medicine physician, commonly called a General Physician, is a medical specialist trained to diagnose, treat, and prevent diseases in adult patients.

They handle everything from routine health check-ups to complex medical conditions involving multiple organs.

ğŸ”¹ Core Responsibilities
What Does a General Physician Do?

A General Physician manages a wide range of medical responsibilities in both hospitals and clinics.

Main Duties

Clinical Diagnosis
Evaluating symptoms through physical exams, patient history, lab tests, and imaging.

Chronic Disease Management
Long-term treatment of conditions like diabetes, hypertension, and heart disease.

Acute Care Treatment
Managing sudden illnesses such as fever, infections, pneumonia, and stomach disorders.

Preventive Healthcare
Vaccinations, routine health screenings, and early disease detection.

Care Coordination
Referring patients to specialists and managing overall treatment plans.
